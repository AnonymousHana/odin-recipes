Japanese Curry

Homemade pork cutlet Japanese curry is a delicious and satisfying dish that is popular in Japan.

List of ingredients

    • 4 pork loin chops
    • 1 onion, chopped
    • 2 carrots, chopped
    • 2 potatoes, peeled and chopped
    • 3 cloves garlic, minced
    • 2 cups water
    • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
    • 1 tablespoon butter
    • 1 tablespoon flour
    • 2 tablespoons curry powder
    • 1 tablespoon garam masala
    • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
    • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
    • Salt and pepper, to taste
    • Cooked rice, for serving

Making the curry:

    Season the pork chops with salt and pepper. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the pork chops to the skillet and cook until browned on both sides, about 3-4 minutes per side. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
    In the same skillet, add the chopped onion and garlic and cook until softened, about 2-3 minutes. Add the chopped carrots and potatoes and cook for another 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    In a separate saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the flour and whisk until smooth. Cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly.
    Add the water to the saucepan and stir until the mixture is smooth. Add the curry powder, garam masala, soy sauce, and Worcestershire sauce. Stir until well combined.
    Add the curry sauce to the skillet with the vegetables and stir until all ingredients are well combined.
    Place the pork chops on top of the curry sauce and vegetables. Cover the skillet with a lid and simmer for 20-30 minutes, or until the pork chops are cooked through and the vegetables are tender.
    Serve the pork cutlet Japanese curry over cooked rice.

And now your Japanese Curry is ready to be eaten! Hooray!
